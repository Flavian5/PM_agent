## Introduction to PCRA

### What is PCRA?

The Project Complexity and Risk Assessment (PCRA) is a Treasury Board-mandated artifact that determines:
- The complexity level of a project (Levels 1-4)
- Whether SSC has the authority to manage a project without Treasury Board oversight
- The appropriate governance and oversight requirements

### PCRA Levels

| Level | Description | Authority |
|-------|-------------|-----------|
| Level 1 | Low complexity | SSC full authority |
| Level 2 | Moderate complexity | SSC full authority |
| Level 3 | High complexity | SSC manages with TBS oversight |
| Level 4 | Very high complexity | Treasury Board approval required |

### When to Complete PCRA

- **Gate 0**: Initial estimate (informational)
- **Gate 1**: Updated PCRA with Investment decision
- **Gate 2**: Full PCRA required for Preliminary Planning approval
- **Gate 3+**: Updated as needed for changes >10%

## Key Concepts and Definitions

### Cost Maturity Levels

Understanding cost maturity is critical for several PCRA questions:

| Level | Accuracy | Use Case |
|-------|----------|----------|
| **ROM (Rough Order of Magnitude)** | ±100% | Early planning, Gate 0-1 |
| **Indicative** | ±40% | Budgeting, Gate 2 |
| **Substantive** | ±10% | Expenditure authority, Gate 3+ |

### Project Stages (PGOF)

| Stage | Gate | Description |
|-------|------|-------------|
| Intake and Concept | Gate 0 | Initial assessment and approval |
| Investment | Gate 1 | Business case development (SSC-led only) |
| Preliminary Planning | Gate 2 | Requirements and strategy definition |
| Detailed Planning | Gate 3 | Full planning and design |
| Execution | Gate 4 | Build, procure, implement |
| Deployment and Closeout | Gate 5 | Transition to operations |

### SSC vs Customer-Led Projects

- **SSC-Led**: 6 stages (0-5), full PCRA required
- **Customer-Led**: 5 stages (0, 2-5), PCRA from client department

### Iterative Gating

For projects with uncertain scope or significant dependencies:
- Projects can iterate through Gates 2, 3, and 4
- PCRA Level 4 projects cannot use iterative gating until post-Gate 3

## Question-by-Question Analysis

### Question 1: Project Priority

**Question**: What level of priority is the project to the organization?

**Options**:
- i. Critical priority: all resources necessary will be allocated to it
- ii. Normal priority: resources may be shared with a project of equal or higher priority

**Key Nuances**:
- "Critical priority" means other projects will be cancelled/postponed to achieve this project
- Different from "high priority" - critical is the highest level
- Must reference Departmental Plan, business case, roadmap, or program documentation

**Required Evidence**:
- Reference to information supporting the selected priority level
- For critical: documentation showing critical importance to SSC mandate/outcomes

**Stage**: Gate 1+ (Section 1 of Business Case)

---

### Question 2: Total Project Cost Estimate

**Question**: What is the Total Project Cost Estimate, including contingency?

**Options** (ranges from $2.5M to $1B+)

**Key Nuances**:
- Must include full costs (GST/HST inclusive)
- Must include Employee Benefit Plans (EBP) - currently 27%
- Must include accommodation costs - currently 13%
- Must include normal contingencies (inflation, foreign exchange)
- Covers Gates 2-5 (definition through closeout)
- Does NOT include initial planning/identification phase costs
- Does NOT include operations and maintenance costs post-project

**Required Evidence**:
- Total fully loaded project cost (Gates 2-5)
- Total investment cost (Gates 0-5) for SSC internal usage
- Reference to source in EPS

**Stage**: Gate 2+ (Section 4 of Business Case)

---

### Question 3: Nature of Cost Estimate

**Question**: What is the nature of the cost estimate for the project?

**Options**:
- 1 - Substantive (±10%)
- 4 - Indicative (±40%)
- 5 - ROM (±100%)

**Key Nuances**:
- Must be supported by a cost report documenting the process
- **ROM**: Ground rules and assumptions clearly documented
- **Indicative**: Cost report with process, rules, assumptions for preliminary key consultations
- **Substantive**: Full cost report with complete key consultations using risk-adjusted activities

**Required Evidence**:
- Cost report referencing the 7-step SSC costing process:
  1. Establish purpose and plan
  2. Create Cost Breakdown Structure (CBS)
  3. Identify ground rules and assumptions
  4. Gather best available evidence
  5. Determine uncertainty, risk and cost
  6. Communicate results in cost report
  7. Assure and validate (by CMA)

**Stage**: Gate 2+ (Section 4 of Business Case)

### Question 6: Susceptibility to Time Delays

**Question**: Is the project susceptible to time delays?

**Options**:
- 1 - No, not susceptible or minimally susceptible (<10% delay)
- 3 - Yes, moderately susceptible (10-20% delay, minor schedule effects)
- 5 - Yes, highly susceptible (>20% delay, major effects, likely requiring revised approvals)

**Causes to Consider**:
- a) Changes in technology
- b) Requirements of participating organizations
- c) Seasonal considerations
- d) Contractor performance (NEW)
- e) Need for policy approvals
- f) External influences

**Key Nuances**:
- If "No/minimally susceptible": explain why
- If "moderately" or "highly": provide estimate of:
  - Potential delay based on risk analysis
  - Impact on key project objectives
  - Source of delays if attributable to other sources

**Required Evidence**:
- Risk statement in EPS if indicating susceptibility
- Reference: PMP Section 2.6 (extract from EPS risk log)

**Stage**: Gate 2+

### Question 7: Project Delivery Methodology

**Question**: Is the project being delivered using a methodology appropriate for expected outcomes?

**Options**:
- a) Yes – Adaptive (Agile)
- b) Yes – Predictive (Waterfall)
- c) Yes – Hybrid

**Key Nuances**:
- Must show how methodology matches project and situation
- **Waterfall**: Suitable for well-defined requirements, stable conditions
- **Agile**: High uncertainty, rapidly changing requirements, frequent adjustments needed
- **Hybrid**: Structured progression with flexibility of iterative design

**Required Evidence**:
- Reference to documented decision process
- Reference: Business Case Section 3.7 (Preferred Option - Delivery Strategy)

**Stage**: Gate 2+

### Question 12: Critical Path Accommodation

**Question**: Does the project schedule accommodate the critical path, including appropriate contingencies?

**Options**:
- Yes/No

**Key Nuances**:

**Required Evidence**:

- Reference: PMP Section 2.5 Schedule
