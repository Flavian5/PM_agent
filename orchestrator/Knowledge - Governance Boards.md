# Governance Boards

## Overview of SSC Governance Boards

| Board | Acronym | Gates Involved | Purpose |
|-------|---------|----------------|---------|
| **5P Board** | People, Process, Planning, Procurement, Project Intake | Gate 0 | Project concept endorsement |
| **Finance and Investment Management Board** | FIMB | Gate 1 (SSC-Led) | Investment approval |
| **Project Management Board** | PMB | Gates 2-5 | Stage progression approval |

---

## 5P Board (People, Process, Planning, Procurement, Project Intake)

### When Engaged
- **Gate 0** - Intake and Concept approval
- As needed throughout project lifecycle for architecture and service authorization decisions

### Decision at Gate 0
- Confirm recommendation that initiative is managed as a gated project
- For SSC-Led >$10M: Confirm Concept Case proceeds to TBS for endorsement
- Approve project to proceed as:
  - SSC-Led project → Preliminary Planning Stage (or Investment Stage)
  - Customer-Led project → Preliminary Planning Stage

### Key Responsibilities
- Review business justification
- High-level scope and cost review
- Confirm client department has authority (Customer-Led)
- Service authorization decisions

### Terms of Reference
- See 5P Board Terms of Reference

---

## FIMB (Finance and Investment Management Board)

### When Engaged
- **Gate 1** - Investment decision (SSC-Led projects ONLY)
- When projects require additional investment

### Decision at Gate 1
- Confirm recommended option warrants initial investment to pursue preliminary planning
- Authorize allocation of funds to advance to Preliminary Planning Stage

### Key Responsibilities
- Review business case
- Validate investment rationale
- Assess expected benefits and costs
- Ensure alignment with SSC strategic objectives

### Terms of Reference
- See Finance and Investment Management Board (FIMB) Terms of Reference

---

## PMB (Project Management Board)

### When Engaged
- **Gate 2** - Preliminary Planning
- **Gate 3** - Detailed Planning
- **Gate 4** - Execution
- **Gate 5** - Deployment and Closeout

### Gate 2 Decision
- Validate delivery strategy for approved option
- Confirm project should continue to be governed within SSC's authorities
- Authorize funds for Detailed Planning Stage

### Gate 3 Decision
- Validate detailed planning sufficient for build/procurement
- Confirm project continues to warrant investment
- Authorize funds for Execution Stage

### Gate 4 Decision
- Validate requirements met to authorize deployment
- Confirm project continues to warrant investment
- Authorize funds for Deployment and Closeout

### Gate 5 Decision
- Confirm project transitioned to Operations
- Confirm closure of internal orders and transfer of assets
- Confirm Business Owner accepted accountability for benefits
- **Approve project closure**

### Key Responsibilities
- Review and approve gate presentations
- Validate business case relevance
- Assess project health and risks
- Ensure alignment with strategic objectives

### Terms of Reference
- See Project Management Board (PMB) Terms of Reference

## Board Comparison

| Aspect | 5P Board | FIMB | PMB |
|--------|----------|------|-----|
| **Frequency** | Gate 0 + as needed | Gate 1 (SSC-Led) | Gates 2-5 |
| **Project Type** | All | SSC-Led only | All |
| **Focus** | Concept, architecture | Investment justification | Delivery and closure |
| **Approval** | Proceed as project | Preliminary planning funds | Stage progression |

## DG Steering Committees (DGSC)

### Best Practices
- Prevent meeting duplication by including partner clients, Client Executives, key stakeholders
- Create single entity for decision-making and information exchange
- Include only necessary decision makers who contribute value
- Clients should attend for project briefings to reduce PM meeting load

### Structure Recommendations
- Assign Chair
- Determine committee member list
- Include individuals who are decision makers and add value

---

## Governance Best Practices

### Meeting Standards
- Schedule regular meetings in advance
- Additional meetings convened as necessary
- Circulate agendas and documentation in advance
- Full participation expected
- Accurate minutes kept and distributed within 1-2 days

### Decision Making
- Decisions based on consensus where possible
- Accountable person responsible for final decision
- Do not initiate meetings unless decisions are intended

### Communication
- Keep audience small
- Clearly define meeting purpose
- Follow standard communication processes
