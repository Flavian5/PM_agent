# Cost Estimating

## Cost Maturity Levels

| Level | Accuracy | When Used | Evidence Required |
|-------|----------|-----------|-------------------|
| **ROM (Rough Order of Magnitude)** | +/- 100% | Gate 0, early scoping | Low - limited information, best estimates |
| **Indicative** | +/- 40% | Gate 1, budgeting | Sufficient - historical data, comparable programs |
| **Substantive** | +/- 10% | Gate 3+, fund release | High - detailed WBS, contracts, signed agreements |

---

## Cost Estimate Requirements by Gate

### Gate 0 (Intake and Concept)
- ROM (+/- 100%) for overall project
- High-level scope and schedule
- Source of funds identified

### Gate 1 (Investment - SSC-Led Only)
- Substantive (+/- 10%) for Preliminary Planning Stage
- ROM (+/- 100%) for remaining project
- Funding sources confirmed

### Gate 2 (Preliminary Planning)
- Substantive (+/- 10%) for Detailed Planning Stage
- ROM (+/- 100%) for remaining project
- Confirmed funding source for next stage

### Gate 3 (Detailed Planning)
- Substantive (+/- 10%) for Execution Stage
- ROM (+/- 100%) for any unplanned iterations
- Confirmed funding for execution

### Gate 4 (Execution)
- Substantive (+/- 10%) for Deployment
- ROM (+/- 100%) for remaining project

## Centrally Managed Costs

### Employee Benefits Plan (EBP)
- **Rate**: 27%
- **Applied to**: All new funding requests after August 1, 2018

### Accommodation
- **Rate**: 13%
- **Note**: If using Occupancy Instrument, cost is accounted as O&M

### SSC IM/IT Costs
- **Rate**: $700 per new FTE per year

---

## Inflation and Escalation

- Use standard rate with justification
- Historical CPI rate from Bank of Canada recommended
- For multiple-year requests, state escalation rate with supporting evidence
- Use recommended rates from latest Bank of Canada Monetary Policy report

---

## Risk and Contingencies

### ROM Level
- Lump sums at manager's discretion
- Ballpark figure used
- Preliminary risk assessment
- Blanket contingency % applied

### Indicative Level
- Preliminary risk assessment performed
- Contingency taken at high level
- Risk Management Plan completed (Low, Med, High with $ value)

### Substantive Level
- Detailed risk assessment required
- Contingency applied based on cost nature
- Probabilistic methods used (Monte Carlo, Sensitivity Analysis)

---

## Cost Categories

### Direct Salaries
- Positions, classification, level of effort
- Pay levels within collective agreement ranges
- Support with reliable sources (actuals, recent data)

### Direct O&M - General
- Historical 3rd party estimates
- Comparable program data
- Draft or final MOUs, SLAs, contracts

### Direct O&M - Professional Services
- Consultants: quantity, # of days, per diem rates (220 days/fiscal)
- Daily rates supported by draft contract

### Capital
- Must meet SSC's Directive on Accounting for Capital Assets
- Majority of costs well-supported with evidence

### Indirect Costs
- Internal Services, Business Line Support (BLS), Technical Services (TS)
- Use ePET for partner projects and service requests
- TB-approved rates for TB submissions

---

## Costing Process (7 Steps)

1. **Establish purpose and plan** - Define reason and timeline
2. **Employ Cost Breakdown Structure (CBS)** - Align with WBS
3. **Identify ground rules and assumptions** - Boundaries and hypotheses
4. **Gather evidence and data** - Quantitative and SME qualitative info
5. **Determine uncertainty, risk and cost** - Approach and fluctuations
6. **Communicate results** - Cost report for CMA review
7. **Assure and validate** - CMA review and endorsement
